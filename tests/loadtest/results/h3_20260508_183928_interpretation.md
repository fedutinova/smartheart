## Context

This is the third and final H3 load-test run. The system now includes three safety layers on top of the hybrid cache:

1. **Hybrid cache** — OR logic: accept a candidate if vector similarity ≥ 0.88 OR trigram similarity ≥ 0.80.
2. **Contradiction guard** — lexical pre-filter: 13 antonym pairs (left/right, hyper/hypo, STEMI/NSTEMI, tachycardia/bradycardia, …). If the incoming and cached questions differ on any guarded axis → force MISS.
3. **LLM judge** — `gpt-4o-mini`, T=0, MaxTokens=5. After the guard passes, asks the model: "Do both questions ask about the same clinical ECG topic and require the same answer?" Prompt includes a named-criteria rule: questions asking about different named diagnostic scoring systems (Cornell vs Sokolow-Lyon) are NOT equivalent. If the answer is not YES → force MISS.

Dataset corrections relative to v2:
- Replaced 3 questions whose normalized form matched fill-set entries directly (dataset contamination): "синдром Бругада" → "синдром Клерка-Леви-Кристеско", "миокардит" → "гипертрофическая кардиомиопатия", "несинусовый ритм" → "желудочковая парасистолия".

## Latency

Cached responses are ~7.5× faster than uncached on this stand (avg 1 052 ms vs 7 901 ms). The P95 ratio is similar (1 503 ms vs 11 612 ms). The judge call adds roughly 1–4 s of overhead when a cache candidate is found and passed by the contradiction guard, but this is still well within the 120 s handler timeout and faster than full RAG.

pg_trgm remains a dead path in runtime: all 29 HITs used `match_method = vector`. The hybrid label is accurate at the schema level (both indexes exist and are queried) but the vector signal always dominates within the OR logic at these thresholds.

## Conclusion

H3 hypothesis is **confirmed** on this dataset.

| Subclaim | Criterion | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Effectiveness | Measure hit rate > 0.30 | **0.52** ✓ |
| Safety | Negative false-hit rate < 0.10 | **0.08** ✓ |

The path from baseline to confirmed hypothesis required three architectural layers rather than one: similarity thresholds alone proved insufficient (no global threshold satisfies both criteria simultaneously), the contradiction guard addressed antonym pairs but left same-domain different-diagnosis cases open, and the LLM judge closed that residual category.

### Design decisions worth documenting

**Why OR logic instead of AND?** AND(vector ≥ 0.88, trigram ≥ 0.80) eliminates nearly all false hits but also kills legitimate reformulation hits — trigram similarity for rephrased questions frequently falls below 0.40. OR casts a wider net and delegates false-positive filtering to the guard and judge layers.

**Why gpt-4o-mini?** The judge task is binary classification with a short context. The model produces consistent YES/NO at T=0 in under 2 s per call. gpt-4o would not improve accuracy on this task and costs ~20× more per token.

**Why a named-criteria rule in the prompt?** Cornell vs Sokolow-Lyon is the archetypal case where two questions share the same clinical goal (LVH diagnosis) but require different specific answers. Without the explicit rule, the judge says YES. Adding one sentence to the prompt resolves this class of errors without affecting reformulation recall.

### Known limitations

1. The LLM judge adds latency to every request that produces a cache candidate. At low cache fill volumes this is acceptable; at high fill this overhead becomes a bottleneck. Caching judge decisions (question-pair → verdict) would eliminate redundant calls.
2. pg_trgm matching never fires in practice. This is not a bug but makes the "hybrid" description somewhat aspirational. A lower trigram threshold (0.60) would activate the pg_trgm path for short paraphrases without many shared trigrams but high structural similarity.
3. The negative dataset is domain-specific (cardiac ECG, Russian language). Generalization to other KB domains or other languages has not been tested.
